Information Security Policy

The purpose of this Information Security Policy is to establish the principles and guidelines that govern the safeguarding of Company's information assets. This policy aims to ensure the confidentiality, integrity, and availability of sensitive information and systems, support incident response efforts, and foster a culture of cybersecurity awareness.

This policy applies to all employees, contractors, vendors, and any other individuals or entities that have access to Company's information assets. It covers all information and information systems owned, operated, or maintained by the company.

  • All information assets must be classified based on their sensitivity and criticality.
  • Access to and disclosure of sensitive information should be limited to authorized personnel only.
  • Data encryption should be used for sensitive and confidential information in transit and at rest.
  • Access rights to information systems and data should be granted on a need-to-know basis.
  • Strong authentication mechanisms, such as two-factor authentication, should be used to verify the identity of users.
  • User access should be regularly reviewed and revoked when no longer required.
  • Our company will maintain an incident response plan that outlines procedures for identifying, reporting, and responding to security incidents.
  • All employees must report any security incidents promptly to the designated incident response team.
  • Regular training and awareness programs will be conducted to educate employees on security best practices.
  • Employees are encouraged to report any security concerns or potential vulnerabilities.
  • The company will adhere to relevant industry standards and regulations, such as ISO 27001.
  • Periodic audits and assessments will be conducted to ensure compliance.

The incident response team will be responsible for managing and responding to security incidents. The IT department will oversee the technical implementation of security measures and monitor for security incidents. Employees are responsible for adhering to this policy and reporting any security incidents or concerns.

Violation of this policy may result in disciplinary actions, including termination of employment or legal action, if applicable.

This policy will be reviewed annually or as required by changes in the business environment, emerging threats, or regulatory requirements. Necessary updates will be made to maintain the policy's effectiveness.

This policy will be maintained and controlled by Policy Management team.

This Information Security Policy is approved by Gabriel Nechifor.

By creating and implementing this Information Security Policy, our Company aims to protect its information assets and promote a culture of security awareness and responsibility throughout the organization. This policy should be communicated to all employees and stakeholders, and training should be provided to ensure understanding and compliance.
